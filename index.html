(A bare room at the Institute for the Advancement of Dictatorships. A long table. At its far side sit the Director and Andrey Vyshinsky. Opposite them, the First Student sits alone. Beside him, an empty chair. At the side of the table, slightly apart, sits the Instructor. A clock can be heard ticking.)

(The Director checks his watch.)

THE DIRECTOR

Vyshinsky, I apologize.

VYSHINSKY

I have time.

THE DIRECTOR

This is not acceptable. Did you waive your right to counsel?

THE FIRST STUDENT

No.

THE DIRECTOR

Three minutes.

(The Director looks at his watch again. Vyshinsky smiles at the First Student. The clock ticks.)

(Three minutes pass.)

THE DIRECTOR

We proceed.
You are charged with aggravated insubordination.
The panel will determine the outcome.
You may be dismissed.
Your scholarship may be revoked.
You may be exonerated.
You may be executed.

(He pauses.)

THE DIRECTOR

The panel will hear the witness first.

THE INSTRUCTOR

We were on a field trip.
We were showcasing important work currently being done.
The First Student did not appreciate it.

THE FIRST STUDENT

I did not.

THE DIRECTOR

You have not been asked to speak.
Display renitence again, and I know the verdict.

THE INSTRUCTOR

He called the work a horror.

THE FIRST STUDENT

I did.

VYSHINSKY

I take it that is strike two.

THE DIRECTOR

Witness, continue.

THE INSTRUCTOR

The Host was not amused.
He asked me to his office later.
It was not…

(He pauses.)

THE INSTRUCTOR

A pleasant experience.

VYSHINSKY

I can picture this. He is not easily amused.

THE DIRECTOR

Nor am I.

(He looks at the First Student.)

THE DIRECTOR

What have you to say in your defense?

THE FIRST STUDENT

The horror.

VYSHINSKY

Strike three.

THE DIRECTOR

The panel is ready to reach a verdict.
Please step outside.

(The Instructor and the First Student stand.)

(A knock on the door. The Caretaker enters.)

THE CARETAKER

It was difficult to wake him. But here he is.

(Napoleon enters.)

NAPOLEON

What an ungodly hour.

THE DIRECTOR

Vyshinsky, Napoleon.
Napoleon, Vyshinsky.

VYSHINSKY

What a pleasure to finally meet.

(Napoleon walks to the First Student and pats him on the back.)

NAPOLEON

What is this trouble?

THE DIRECTOR

Please all sit again.

VYSHINSKY

I wish I could have spoken to you in 1812.

NAPOLEON

It is after six? I thought it was morning.

(He sits, stretching out his legs.)

NAPOLEON

I found this.

(He takes out a small piece of metal.)

THE DIRECTOR

What is this?

THE INSTRUCTOR

It is a fragment of an MK-84.

THE DIRECTOR

A formidable bomb.

THE FIRST STUDENT

I heard their voices.

VYSHINSKY

Do I still have to count?

THE DIRECTOR

I count four in the last fifteen minutes. I am ready to reach a verdict.

VYSHINSKY

Shall we confer?

THE DIRECTOR

Yes. We confer here and now. And swiftly. What is your recommendation, Vyshinsky?

VYSHINSKY

If there is justice, he will soon be executed.

NAPOLEON

Yes, he must be exonerated.

(The First Student stands.)

(Napoleon looks at the fragment again.)
